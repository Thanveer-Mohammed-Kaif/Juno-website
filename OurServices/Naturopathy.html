Treatments using Fasting Therapy

Fasting therapy involves voluntarily abstaining from food (and sometimes drink) for a specific period to promote physical and mental health benefits. This practice is based on the belief that temporarily reducing food intake can help the body detoxify, repair, and rejuvenate itself. Common types of fasting therapy include intermittent fasting, where eating is restricted to a certain window of time each day, and periodic fasting, which involves going without food for one or more days at a time.

Treatments using Oil Pulling

Oil pulling is a traditional oral health practice that involves swishing a tablespoon of oil (commonly coconut, sesame, or sunflower oil) in your mouth on an empty stomach for about 15-20 minutes, then spitting it out. This practice is rooted in Ayurvedic medicine, a holistic healing system from India. The process is believed to draw out toxins from the body, improve oral health, and potentially offer other health benefits.

Treatments using Walking Therapy

Walking therapy, often referred to as therapeutic walking, is a simple and accessible form of therapy that combines the physical activity of walking with mental and emotional support. It can be practiced independently or guided by a therapist who engages in talk therapy while walking with the client in a natural or calm setting.

The idea is that the act of walking, especially in nature, can help to clear the mind, reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression, stimulate creativity, and improve physical health. The rhythmic nature of walking helps to relax and focus the mind, making it easier for individuals to open up about their feelings and thoughts. Walking therapy is beneficial for people of all ages and fitness levels, offering both the mental health benefits of psychotherapy and the physical health benefits of moderate exercise.

Treatments using Bathing Therapy

Bathing therapy, also known as balneotherapy, involves using water for therapeutic purposes, leveraging its temperature and sometimes added minerals to promote physical and mental well-being. This treatment can include soaking in hot springs, mineral baths, mud baths, or simply warm baths at home.

1. Body bathing methods: The entire body is wrapped in a wet sheet and then in a dry covering. Specific instructions are followed regarding the process of treatment. The body sweats profusely and needs to be monitored continuously.

2. Foot bathing methods : Foot or leg is immersed in a tub of water at specific temperature. There are hot as well as cold foot baths.

3. Sun bathing methods : Sun bathing involves exposing the body to sunlight in controlled doses to harness the benefits of natural light, including Vitamin D synthesis, which supports bone health, immune function, and mood improvement.

Treatments using Clapping Therapy

Clapping therapy is a form of holistic healing that involves rhythmically clapping the hands, believed to stimulate specific acupressure points and improve circulation. This practice is thought to activate the body’s natural healing processes, releasing tension and enhancing energy flow. Advocates suggest it can boost immunity, reduce stress, and even improve heart health by increasing blood circulation. Additionally, the rhythmic sounds and physical activity may uplift mood and promote a sense of well-being.
